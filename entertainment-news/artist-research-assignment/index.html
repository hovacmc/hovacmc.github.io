Artist research project

An example of Cody Lecoy's artwork, Cody named these both as Encode, they are worth 3,500$. These both were acrylics done on a canvas and are 14" x 18" each. This shows a tadpol entering a tunnel and leaving as a frog, according to Cody Lecoy it represents "With the advent of new technology, I reflect upon what is gained and what is left behind as we live through a technological metamorphosis." www.lattimergallery.com, Mandatory Credit

Luka Maljevic, Student of 8DFI
Published Wednesday, January 20, 2021 8PM EST
Last Updated Wednesday, January 25, 2021 7PM EST

Biography:

Cody Lecoy is an artist of Sylix and Lekwungen descent. He was born in Richmond, BC in Canada on May 16, 1989. Cody studied at Kwantlen Polytechnic University of Fine Arts Program in Vancouver in 2008 and was a recipient of the Vancouver International Airport Art Foundation Scholarship in 2011 and 2012. Cody has been featured in many galleries and exhibitons such as the Richmond Art Gallery and Lattimer Gallery.

Cody continues to work on his art career and sells his paintings. Some for sale work as of today are Encode, Perspective, Culture and Dissociation.


Artwork:

This is an artwork by Cody Lecoy called Renewal. This artwork has a mix of cold and hot colours, the cold ones being on in the sky while the ground and the animal in it are hot colours. This artwork's size is big, as it is 24" x 30". The technique used is surrealism. This artwork represents rejuvenation and decay, specifically in regards to fossil fuels according to Cody. I believe this is one of his most famous artworks as it was one of the first ones I found on the internet and it was sold in 2015.


Analysis:

Cody Lecoy's artwork touches topics around the environment and nature with themes such as renewable energy, oil and gas usage. I believe this artist is very pationate about his artwork and it's message about nature. It ties in with his aboriginal ancestry. He often talks about sustainability and technology and how it has a great costs to the environment. Through his artwork I see this message. Here are some more examples of his work:

Web Navigation

The colours used are cold and neutral colours. This artwork's size is 23 1/2" x 31". The technique used is surrealism. According to Cody Lecoy, the message is about the adaptability and agileness of the squirells in an urban environment, it represents technology and its advantages/dangers of it.


Undertow

The colours used are cold and hot colours. This artwork's size is 24" x 30". The technique used is surrealism. According to Cody Lecoy, the message is about animals that exist in between the realms of existance, some of these animals are beavers, frogs and other animals he frequently paints.


GM-Maize

The colours used are all types of colours. This artwork's size is 12" x 9". The technique used is surrealism. According to Cody Lecoy, the painting examines the results of a rat fed on corn with GMOs, and how GMOs can alter the bodily functions that arent understood yet.



Influences:

I think the artist's life and experiences influences their work in many ways. Cody is an aboriginal and has a strong connection with animals and nature, his art is all about the environment and nature and how they are being affected. Most of his work is acrylic on canvas and he uses surrealism, which lets him express himself and his experiences.


Artist statement:

In his artist statement, Cody explains how through painting he can bring balance to the mind, body and spirit. He also talks about how the creativity in art allows him to have a deeper understanding of his indigenous, Sylix and Lekwungen ancestry. He expresses himself with surrealism, as it lets him portray the interior of live and external environmeent on the canvas. I believe his message is nature and how energy, oil and gas affects it. I think this is his message because in a lot of his artwork it shows animals with factories and smoke in the background.


Personal Connection:

Before this assignment I didnt know who Cody Lecoy was, I personally really like his work as it touches very real and important topics while looking really good. His artwork is very different than others, I like his work a lot.
